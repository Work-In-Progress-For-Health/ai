## What the data shows

Ten deployments over roughly seven years works out to one deploy every eight to nine months on average. In the DORA framework's classic four-tier model (elite / high / medium / low), anything slower than once every six months falls off the bottom of the "low" bucket entirely. Taken at face value, this is the slowest possible classification.

A few patterns worth noting before drawing conclusions:

The versioning scheme changed partway through. Early versions look semver-ish with large build numbers appended (`2.1.29108`, `2.1.31337`), then switched to CalVer (`2022.3.1`, `2025.4.010`). That's usually a signal of a process or org change — a re-architecture, a new release policy, a team reorg, or an acquisition.

A 2022.4.3 version was deployed in February 2024 — roughly 14 months after the 2022.4 line would have been "current." That suggests either long-lived LTS/maintenance branches, slow customer upgrade cycles, or a gap between "cut a release" and "deploy a release."

Recent cadence has stabilized at something close to annual. The last four intervals are 14, 15, 11, and ~11 months. That's not chaos — that's a rhythm. Someone somewhere decided on yearly releases.

## The caveat that matters most

**DORA's thresholds were calibrated on SaaS and internet services**, where shipping to production is fully under the team's control. They translate poorly to: on-prem enterprise software, installable desktop apps, firmware/embedded, mobile apps with app-store review cycles, regulated industries (medical, aerospace, finance core), or anything shipped on physical media. If this is a packaged product that customers install, a yearly major release is *normal and often desirable* — customers don't want continuous churn. Telling that team they're "low-performing" by DORA would be wrong.

So the first question isn't "how do we deploy more?" — it's "does DORA even apply here?"

## Questions to ask before recommending anything

- Is this a SaaS service you control end-to-end, or a product shipped to customers?
- Does "deployment" here mean production deploy, or release tag / version published? These are different metrics.
- Are you also tracking the other three DORA metrics (lead time for changes, change failure rate, mean time to restore)? Deployment frequency alone is misleading — a team could deploy daily with awful quality, or yearly with pristine reliability.
- What drove the versioning scheme change around 2022? Does that correspond to a process shift?
- Why was 2022.4.3 deployed in 2024 — LTS backport, slow customer rollout, or something else?
- Is there a gap between internal deploys (staging, canary) and what's captured in this list?

## Recommendations — if DORA does apply

Assuming this is a service where faster deployment is genuinely valuable: the typical levers are trunk-based development, feature flags to decouple deploy from release, investment in CI/CD automation, smaller batch sizes, and automated testing to reduce the fear tax on each deploy. But I'd resist prescribing any of these until the context is clearer — applying SaaS playbooks to a shipped-product team causes real harm (rushed releases, unhappy customers, burnt-out engineers).

## The honest insight

Ten data points spanning seven years, with a version-scheme change in the middle, is too little signal to diagnose a team. What the data *does* tell you clearly: cadence has been roughly yearly for the last three years, and it's consistent. Whether that's a problem depends entirely on what this system is and who depends on it.
